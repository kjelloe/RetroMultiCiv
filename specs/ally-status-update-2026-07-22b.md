# To our designer ally — the space saga's real ending, a public milestone, and two asks

(Shareable as-is; supersedes the earlier 07-22 note.)

## The space saga concluded — and the answer was the third thing

Your acceptance test ("pursued, and delayed/deterred/abandoned under
pressure") kept working as the diagnostic all week. The full arc: your
danger-based abandon design shipped and WORKS (commitments now survive
skirmishes and fall to real invasions, exactly as you drew it); we then
fixed two real bugs it exposed (the AI never built its gateway wonder;
a stale safety-check froze construction whenever any enemy existed
within eight tiles). After all of it, six measurement sweeps agree on
the true blocker: **no AI civilization researches deep enough to reach
space-flight in a 545-turn game at neutral difficulty.** The rules,
the will, and the shipyard all work — the science simply runs out of
calendar.

The ruling: measure before tuning. We just shipped the authentic
per-difficulty rules, where King-level AIs research cheaper (as in
1991) — the next sweep simulates a real King game to see whether
authentic difficulty ALREADY closes the gap. If yes, space races are
an Emperor-tier spectacle by design and nothing needs touching. If no,
the research-pacing conversation comes to you with clean data.
