# To our designer ally — your designs shipped, and one earned a saga

(Shareable as-is. The overnight-and-morning report.)

## Your event designs are now engine truth

- **"Foreign sails spotted off the coast"** — shipped exactly as you drew
  it: barbarian sea raids land with your one-turn telegraph, visibility-
  gated (a rival can't see YOUR incoming raid), and raiders seek capture,
  not loot. Island players finally live in dangerous times.
- **The eight classic disasters** are in (authentic-ON with a mercy
  toggle), and every disaster event carries its PREVENTION building —
  the data hook your "lesson-first" crisis cards need. The cards
  themselves are next in the client queue.
- **The full nuclear detonation** landed too: everything on the tile
  dies, the city halves, the ring is fouled — with the Manhattan Project
  gate queued to complete the picture.
- Pollution, global warming, and the meltdown shipped the same day.

## The space-race saga — your acceptance test caught three bugs

Your one-line acceptance — "at least one civilization recognizes and
pursues the Space route; under pressure it can be delayed, deterred, or
forced to abandon" — turned out to be a diagnostic instrument. Three
measurement sweeps, three findings:
1. The AI abandoned projects over ANY disturbance (an every-turn peace
   check). We tuned tolerance — better, still failing.
2. Commits then survived to 91% research completion but died to threat
   SPIKES. We added patience (sustained-threat hysteresis) — still
   failing.
3. The decisive data: every abandoning civilization had suffered ZERO
   military losses. The "threat" signal was proximity anxiety, not
   danger.
The ruling (this morning): abandonment now triggers only on REAL events —
a city actually lost, the capital actually attacked, or full war. Your
"delayed, deterred, or forced to abandon" stays true; imagined danger no
longer counts. Fourth measurement runs after the change. This is your
design philosophy — measured behavior over asserted behavior — working
exactly as intended.
