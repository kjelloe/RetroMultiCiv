# Game concept brief — for branding consultation

_Prepared for an external gaming-branding specialist. Context: the game has
shipped its 1.0 under the working title **RetroMultiCiv**; this
consultation is about its permanent public identity. Our designer has a
worked-out position (summarized at the end) — we are seeking professional
validation, challenge, or a better answer._

## The game, in one paragraph

A browser-based civilization-building strategy game in the lineage of the
original 1991 Sid Meier's Civilization — rebuilt faithfully, not
imitated loosely. One player or several guide a civilization from founding
its first settlement in 4000 BC to, potentially, launching a colony ship to
Alpha Centauri around 2100 — roughly 545 turns of exploration, city
building, research, diplomacy, war, and statecraft. It runs in any browser
with nothing to install, on desktop, tablet, and phone, and also ships as a
native Roblox experience built on the same deterministic game engine.

## What makes it distinctive (the substance behind the brand)

1. **Authenticity as a design ethic.** Rules match the 1991 original
   wherever the original can be verified — down to restoring rules most
   remakes drop (settlers cost population; triremes gamble on open water;
   disasters strike unprepared cities). Where we deviate, we label it.
   The game has a documented provenance system for every rule.
2. **A living AI with legible intentions.** Opponents manage economies,
   negotiate peace, break treaties with consequences, and — when safe and
   scientifically ahead — deliberately race for the space victory. A human
   can SEE the launch threat forming and choose to race, pressure, or
   invade. Behavior is tuned by large-scale simulation, not scripts.
3. **True multiplayer, two ways.** Browser players host or join games over
   LAN/internet (join codes, reconnection, AI regency minding absent
   players); Roblox players get the same game natively. A public server
   directory (the classic QuakeWorld pattern) lets anyone host.
4. **Deterministic to the byte.** Every game can be replayed exactly from
   its command log — across two independent engine implementations
   (JavaScript and Roblox Luau). This is unusual engineering; as brand
   material it translates to: fair, verifiable, cheat-resistant.
5. **Europe-first by design.** Hosted in the EU; no accounts, no
   analytics, no trackers; game saves are files the operator owns. The
   privacy posture is real practice, not a badge.
6. **A procedural visual identity.** The game generates its own art: 68
   per-technology glyphs (era-framed motifs), per-civilization visual
   languages, three world styles on Roblox (including a toy-brick
   "Studded" mode). There is a coherent visual system a brand could build
   on — no licensed art anywhere.

## Audience and tone

Players who loved 1991-era grand strategy and want it *actually good
again* — plus their kids on Roblox, which is not a port but a first-class
citizen. Sessions are long and thoughtful; multiplayer is social
(friends + join codes, not matchmaking ladders). The game's voice — in its
advisor hints, encyclopedia, and event text — is friendly, factual, and a
little grave; never sarcastic. Think "a good history teacher who lets you
break things."

## Naming: where we stand

- Working title **RetroMultiCiv** did honest development duty and is
  retired from player-facing use (engineering label; invites "Civ-clone"
  framing; unpronounceable in a sentence).
- Our designer's front-runner: **Founders** — one-word player-verb,
  covers hut-to-starship, passes the say-it-aloud test ("Let's play
  Founders tonight"). Reserve list: Firstlight, Worldmakers,
  Commonwealth, Civil Dawn, and five more on file.
- Constraints we believe in: the name must work as a domain, a Roblox
  experience title, a phone tab, and a spoken sentence. Avoid:
  Civ/Civilization, Empire(s), generic "Age of…", war-only framing, and
  implementation words (Retro/Multi/Online).
- Domain thinking: [title].eu as canonical home (matches the real
  European hosting/privacy posture), .no and .com as redirects, total
  ~€26/yr; the €448/yr .game TLD rejected as vanity burn.

## What we are asking the specialist

1. Does **Founders** survive professional scrutiny (distinctiveness,
   trademark exposure in EU + key markets, platform collision, search
   findability for an indie title)?
2. If not — critique of the reserve list against the constraints above,
   or a direction we have not considered.
3. Is the .eu-canonical domain strategy sound for an indie game with
   Roblox reach, or does it undercut discoverability?
4. Anything about the identity system (procedural glyphs, the era-framed
   visual language) worth formalizing into the brand rather than leaving
   as in-game art?

## Practical notes

- Budget reality: this is an independent project; identity spend competes
  with hosting and playtesting. Recommendations should scale.
- Whatever the name outcome, internal format identifiers keep the old
  codename permanently (a technical compatibility guarantee) — renaming
  is a display/domain event, deliberately cheap.
- The current public home (multiciv.kjell.today) remains the test/dev
  tier under any outcome.
