## Where your design eye would help most

A handful of the new items are design questions more than code questions.
No obligation — but if any spark ideas, we'd love your take:

1. **Tech-discovery celebration.** Discovering a technology currently shows
   a small message. Planned: a big overlay — the tech's icon art, a ~3-second
   fanfare, the Civilopedia article, then "Close" / "Select research". What
   should the MOMENT feel like? (Civ 1's little council scenes are the
   ancestral reference.)
2. **A third Roblox world style: "Studded" / "Brick".** Vibrant, flat,
   hyper-saturated colors; studded blocks; bevelled brick geometry — the
   toy-brick look popular on Roblox. If you have opinions on how RetroCiv
   terrain/units/cities should translate into that language (what stays
   readable at a glance, what becomes charmingly blocky), sketch away.
   One hard rule: we name it "Studded"/"Brick" style — never the L-word
   brand.
3. **Terrain relief.** Playtest feedback: grassland/plains read as "hilly"
   when they should read flat. We're flattening flats and raising hills for
   contrast (capped well below mountains). Any art direction on how much
   relief reads best from the top-down view is welcome.
4. **Friendly first-page hints.** The start screen gets a "?" overlay with
   arrow callouts explaining "Start Game" / "LAN game" / "Join game" — in a
   friendly voice. If you'd enjoy writing those three little texts, they're
   yours; otherwise we'll draft and you can red-pen.
