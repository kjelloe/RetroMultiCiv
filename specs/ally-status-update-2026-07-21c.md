runs in the game.)

## The space-project AI shipped — and the first data is a delight

Your slice design went from sketch to shipped in about a day: the
eligibility gate, the deliberate commit, research that steers toward the
spaceship path, gold flowing into components (never wonders — the 1991
rule holds), and your contestability requirement — a threatened program
pauses; a broken one is abandoned.

The first measurement through your 9-metric instrument is the kind of
result designers dream about: in our reference game, a civilization
reached eligibility at turn 290 with 70% of the research path done — and
**never committed, because it was never safe enough**. That is your
"space must demand a visible tradeoff" working as measured behavior, not
as a slogan. The multi-seed sweep (your full metric table) runs next.

## The best story of the night: authenticity beat the metrics, twice

1. We found our AI's settlers had been literally walking into walls for
   weeks (a pathing bug at coastal inlets). Fixing it TRIPLED measured
   expansion — every AI-quality floor went green.
2. Hours later we restored a missing 1991 rule: settlers cost a
   population point (a size-1 city building one becomes the settler and
   disbands). Expansion promptly halved — the authentic price returned,
   and our shiny new metrics dropped back down.

We ruled authenticity the winner both times: the metrics get re-baselined
to the now-correct game rather than the game being tuned to old metrics.
Your kind of call, we suspect.
